A model we believe in

Steward Ownership

Steward ownership shifts control from investors to the people who run a business — putting everyone on the same side of the table. For us, it isn't philanthropy. They are investments that make our capital enabling, not extractive.

In a typical landlord–tenant arrangement, the two sides are often at odds. Steward ownership removes that tension: the people closest to the work hold the power to run it, and the property is protected for its mission rather than for profit.

How our model works for businesses

  1. 1

    We purchase a building property.

  2. 2

    The building is renovated to make it ready for its purpose.

  3. 3

    The people who operate the business — the stewards — take over and run it themselves.

  4. 4

    Stewards pay only actual carrying costs of the building property — taxes, mortgage, and insurance — and nothing more. We as investors take no return on our capital.

  5. 5

    If we as investors and the stewards all agree to sell the property (and it is approved by the Golden Share holder), we split the upside of the sale 50/50 with the stewards.

The rights we give up

As investors, we deliberately give up rights that would normally be ours. As long as the stewards keep paying the carrying costs and stay true to the project's agreed-upon mission, we cannot mortgage or sell the building property out from under the stewards. That security is the point — it lets stewards commit fully, knowing the ground won't shift underneath them.

The Golden Share

Each project also includes a Golden Share — a special share held by someone whose only role is to ensure the mission is maintained. It's a lasting safeguard that keeps a project true to its purpose, no matter who comes and goes.

Why it matters

This is a model for the next generation. As a vast transfer of wealth gets underway, many who inherit it want to put their money to work for good — not to extract, and not simply to give it all away. Steward ownership offers a third path: capital that is enabling, building long-lasting mission-driven businesses, with the people who do the work holding the power.

We're putting it into practice with projects like Doves Diner, Casa Latina, La Morada, and BuildHudson.
